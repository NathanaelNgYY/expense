# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

A single person (plus occasional shared-budget members) tracking personal spending day-to-day on their phone: logging expenses in a few taps, checking "can I still spend today," and reviewing the month at a glance. Used in short, frequent bursts (checking a balance before a purchase), not long sessions.

## Product Purpose

A mobile-first expense tracker: log spend, see category budgets (Lunch, Transport, Savings, Investments, Others), a monthly buffer, and a spend forecast/safe-to-spend-per-day figure. Also supports shared budgets between multiple people and a "Poker" session ledger. Success = the user trusts the numbers enough to make a spend decision from the dashboard without digging into History.

## Brand Personality

Bold & confident. Big, unambiguous numbers; one strong accent color driving state (on-budget vs over); minimal ornamentation. References: Cash App, Robinhood — money as the hero, not the chrome around it.

## Anti-references

Not a cluttered spreadsheet-in-app-clothing. Not a flat, gray, enterprise SaaS dashboard. Not overly playful/cutesy (no mascot illustrations) — this is about trust in numbers, not delight for its own sake.

## Design Principles

- The number is the interface — amount, category spend, and safe-to-spend read instantly, not after parsing a chart.
- One accent, used deliberately — color communicates budget state (under/over), it isn't decoration.
- Dark by default — the app already runs on a black/near-black iOS-system palette; direction changes should sharpen that, not abandon it for a light SaaS look.
- Fast entry above all — Add Entry is a numpad-first flow; nothing should slow down logging a spend.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum, especially for the red/green/yellow budget-state colors (don't rely on hue alone — pair with icons/labels already in place). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for entry animations.
