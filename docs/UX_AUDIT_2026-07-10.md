# UX Audit — Budget Tracker PWA

**Date:** 2026-07-10
**Method:** Playwright-driven inspection of the running app at 375×667, 375×812, 390×844, 430×932
(populated, empty, and filled-form states), plus source review.
**Build audited:** `original-dark` theme, personal (non-shared) mode.
**Scope note:** No code was changed for this audit. Two CSS files (`src/index.css`, `src/themes.css`)
carry an unrelated, previously-requested fix to Add Entry spacing.

Findings are marked **[Confirmed]** (observed in the running app or read in source) or
**[Assumption]** (inferred intent, stated as such).

---

## A. Executive Summary

| Dimension | Score | One-line justification |
|---|---|---|
| Overall UX | **5 / 10** | Fast core loop, but the primary number on the home screen is unlabeled and the app never tells the user when a save fails. |
| User flows | **5 / 10** | "Add expense" is excellent. Every other flow — edit, backfill, budget setup, automation setup — is buried, duplicated, or absent. |
| Mobile usability | **6 / 10** | Bottom nav and thumb-zone Save are right. Touch targets, tab-label size, and the Settings scroll are wrong. |

### The five biggest UX problems

1. **The home screen's biggest number has no label.** `Dashboard.tsx` renders a hero card reading
   `Personal / July 2026 / S$1,000,681` with no word saying whether that is spent, remaining, or
   income. In a financial app this is the single most damaging ambiguity — a user cannot tell a good
   month from a catastrophic one at a glance. **[Confirmed]**

2. **Failed saves are silent.** `EntriesContext.tsx` has three bare `catch {}` blocks (lines 52, 74,
   123). There is no offline banner, no sync indicator, and no retry affordance for personal entries
   — `.offline-banner` exists in CSS but is only ever rendered by `sharedBudgets/SharedScreen.tsx:22`.
   The user taps Save, the app navigates home confidently, and the entry may never reach the server.
   For a money app, this destroys trust the moment it's noticed. **[Confirmed]**

3. **"History" is not history.** The History tab opens on a calendar heatmap, then a full
   **"Add missed expense" form** — a second, differently-designed entry form (plain text amount field,
   not the numpad). The actual transaction list sits roughly 1,600px down the scroll. The tab's label
   promises a list and delivers a data-entry screen. **[Confirmed]**

4. **Two of the five primary nav slots are features you plan to cut.** `docs/APP_STORE.md` removes the
   poker tracker and `src/sharedBudgets/` from the App Store build. Poker — a niche side-feature —
   currently occupies 20% of the app's primary navigation, permanently visible to every user.
   **[Confirmed]**

5. **Currency formatting is inconsistent and breaks on negatives.** 46 call sites use raw
   `toFixed(2)`; only two use `toLocaleString`. Result: the hero prints `S$1,000,681` while the row
   beneath prints `S$1000012.89`. Negative buffer renders as `S$-999776.89 left` — sign in the wrong
   place, and the word "left" is wrong when the value is negative. **[Confirmed]**

### The five strongest parts

1. **The amount-first numpad on Add Entry.** Amount is the only required field; category and note are
   explicitly optional. Save is in the thumb zone. This is the correct shape for a
   log-a-purchase-in-four-seconds app, and most budgeting apps get it wrong.
2. **Delete has real Undo,** not a confirmation dialog (`History.tsx:321`, `:651`). This is the
   better pattern — it never interrupts the common case and still protects the rare mistake.
3. **Optimistic local write + background sync.** `addEntry` commits locally and navigates instantly
   (`AddEntry.tsx:91`, with a comment explaining the choice). The app feels instant offline.
4. **Backfilling from a calendar heatmap is a genuinely good idea.** Tapping a day to fill it in is
   more intuitive than a date picker. It is in the wrong place, but the idea is sound.
5. **Deliberate motion craft.** The per-digit amount animation has a documented reduced-motion
   fallback and avoids `filter: blur()` for iOS performance (`index.css:443`). Rare discipline.

## B. Screen-by-Screen Findings

### B1. Dashboard / Home — `src/screens/Dashboard.tsx`

**Purpose:** Answer "how am I doing this month?"

**What works:** Month + income in the header. Category rows with progress bars and "left" amounts are
scannable. Expandable rows keep detail out of the way until asked for.

**What is confusing**
- **[Confirmed]** The hero card shows a large copper figure (`S$1,000,681` in my seeded data) with
  the labels `Personal` and `July 2026` — but nothing names the *number*. It is total spent.
  Everything below it talks about money *remaining*. The user must reverse-engineer the meaning.
- **[Confirmed]** The hero card is a **green gradient** (`.pass`, `index.css:114`) — the only green
  surface in an otherwise charcoal app. It reads as a leftover from a previous theme, and green
  connotes "good" regardless of whether the month is going well.
- **[Confirmed]** The hero's progress bar has no scale, no endpoint label, and fills completely at
  any large value. At 100% it looks identical to 800%.
- **[Confirmed]** Negative buffer: `S$-999776.89 left`. Sign placement and the word "left" are both
  wrong.
- **[Confirmed]** Mixed number formatting within one viewport: `S$1,200`, `S$1,000,681`,
  `S$1000012.89`.

**Layout problems**
- Buffer is given a full card of equal weight to the hero, but it is a *derived* value most users will
  never reason about. It outranks the category list, which is what people actually check.
- Category rows are ordered by the config object, not by anything the user cares about (spend,
  % used, or overspend).

**Button placement problems**
- **[Confirmed]** Settings is a 34×34px gear in the top-right — below the 44px minimum, and in the
  hardest corner to reach one-handed on a 6.7" phone.

**Missing states**
- **[Confirmed]** Empty state is a dashboard of zeros. No "add your first expense" call to action, no
  explanation, no path forward. A new user's first screen is five rows of `S$0.00`.
- No loading state; no sync/offline state (see B6).

**Recommended changes**
1. Label the hero: `Spent this month` above the figure, and add `S$X left of S$1,200` beneath it.
   Better still, make the hero **the number people want** — money remaining — and demote spent.
2. Retire the green `.pass` gradient; use the app's own surface. Reserve green/red for
   under/over semantics only.
3. Extract one `formatSGD(value)` helper and route all 46 `toFixed(2)` sites through it. Render
   negatives as `S$999,776.89 over`, never `S$-… left`.
4. Empty state: replace the zeroed dashboard with a single card — "Log your first expense" + button
   that jumps to Add.
5. Grow the gear to a 44×44 hit area (`::before { inset: -5px }` keeps the 34px visual).

**Priority: Critical** (hero label, formatting) / **High** (empty state, gear target)

### B2. Add Entry — `src/screens/AddEntry.tsx`

**Purpose:** Log a purchase in seconds.

**What works:** Amount-first numpad; optional category and note; Save in the thumb zone; instant
optimistic save. Genuinely the best screen in the app.

**What is confusing**
- **[Confirmed]** There is no date control. Every entry is `toLocalDateString()` — today. To log
  yesterday's coffee the user must discover that the *History* tab contains a second entry form. No
  affordance on this screen hints at that.
- **[Confirmed]** No Cancel / Clear. Typing `1234` by mistake requires four backspace taps.
- **[Assumption]** `Category (optional)` — the word "optional" is doing double duty as permission and
  as discouragement. Uncategorised entries fall into a `Uncategorized` bucket that appears on the
  dashboard; users won't know that until it happens.

**Layout problems**
- *(Fixed in the pending diff — was: note input flush against Save with 0px gap, uniform 12px rhythm
  throughout, dead band above the tab bar.)* Verified at four viewports post-fix: no overflow, Save
  always above the fold, 11–14px note→Save.

**Button placement problems**
- **[Confirmed]** Category chips are 38px tall — under the 44px minimum, and they sit directly above
  the note field and Save, in the densest part of the thumb zone.
- **[Confirmed]** No heading element on this screen at all; `ADD ENTRY` is a `<p class="screen-title">`.

**Missing states**
- No feedback on save beyond navigation. No toast, no "Saved to Lunch" confirmation.
- Disabled Save gave no reason why. *(Contrast fixed in the pending diff — was ~2:1.)*

**Recommended changes**
1. Add a **date affordance**: a `Today ▾` chip beside the title that opens a compact picker
   defaulting to today. This kills the entire reason the History backfill form exists.
2. Add a `Clear` text button next to the amount when `amount > 0`.
3. Chips to 44px min-height.
4. On save, show a brief toast — `Saved · Lunch · S$5.80` with an Undo — instead of a silent
   navigation. This is also the natural place to surface a sync failure.

**Priority: High**

### B3. History — `src/screens/History.tsx`

**Purpose:** Ostensibly, review past transactions. Actually: backfill, review, analyse, search.

This screen is doing four jobs and leads with the wrong one.

**Current vertical order:** month nav → calendar heatmap → caption → **Add missed expense form** →
Weekly Spending → Month Review → Insights → Entries (search + filter + list).

**What is confusing**
- **[Confirmed]** A user tapping "History" to find a transaction lands on a data-entry form and must
  scroll ~1,600px past three analytics blocks to reach the list.
- **[Confirmed]** The backfill form uses a **plain text `amount-input`**, while Add Entry uses a
  **numpad**. Two different input methods for the identical task, in one app.
- **[Confirmed]** The calendar uses two different ring treatments — white border = selected,
  copper border = today — and needs an 11px caption to explain itself
  (`lighter = heavier spend day · ring = today · tap a day to backfill it`), at 4.15:1 contrast.
  A legend explaining your visualisation is the visualisation failing.
- **[Confirmed]** `Day pattern: Mostly Wednesdays` was generated from 7 entries. Insights computed
  over trivial samples read as noise and erode confidence in the numbers that *do* matter.
- **[Confirmed]** Month Review says `Others led the month | Peak day Jul 6`. The pipe separator is a
  developer's join, not a sentence.

**Button placement problems**
- **[Confirmed]** `month-nav-btn` (prev/next month) are 34×34.
- **[Confirmed]** Filter chips are 34px (`chip--compact`).
- **[Confirmed]** The entry list is the only place to edit or delete; both require first scrolling
  past everything else.
- **[Confirmed]** `App.tsx:23` declares `entryToEditId` and passes `initialEditingEntryId` /
  `onEditHandled` to History — but **nothing ever sets it to a non-null value**. The deep-link-to-edit
  path is dead code.

**Missing states**
- No pagination or virtualisation. **[Assumption]** At a few thousand entries this list will jank;
  at SGD-tracking volumes (~100/month) it's fine for years. Low priority.

**Recommended changes**
1. **Lead with the list.** Order: search/filter → entries → (collapsed) analytics → calendar.
2. **Delete the "Add missed expense" form entirely.** Move date selection into Add Entry (B2.1).
   Keep the calendar as a *jump-to-date* filter for the list, not an entry surface.
3. If the form must stay, make it use the numpad. Never two input methods for one task.
4. Suppress insights below a sample threshold (e.g. `< 15 entries` or `< 3 distinct days`).
5. Rewrite `Others led the month | Peak day Jul 6` → `You spent most on Others. Biggest day: Sat, Jul 6.`

**Priority: Critical** (IA inversion) / **High** (duplicate form)

### B4. Poker — `src/screens/Poker.tsx`, `LogSession.tsx`

**Purpose:** Track poker session P&L. Unrelated to budgeting.

**What is confusing**
- **[Confirmed]** `+S$0.00` renders in green with a `+` sign at zero sessions. Zero is neither a win
  nor positive.
- **[Confirmed]** Occupies a primary nav slot, and `docs/APP_STORE.md` cuts it from the App Store build.

**What works:** The empty state names its own CTA (`No sessions yet. Tap Log Session to start.`) and
the button is bottom-anchored. This is the best empty state in the app — copy the pattern elsewhere.

**Recommended changes**
1. Move out of primary nav (see §E). Neutral colour and no sign at zero.

**Priority: Medium** (High if App Store release is near)

### B5. Shared Budgets — `src/sharedBudgets/SharedScreen.tsx`

**What is confusing**
- **[Confirmed]** The empty state is a bare sign-in wall: a title, one sentence, and
  `Continue with Google`. It never says what a shared budget is, why you'd want one, or what Google
  gets access to. In a financial app, asking for OAuth before explaining value is the highest-drop-off
  screen you can build.
- **[Confirmed]** No heading element on the screen.

**Recommended changes**
1. Explain before authenticating: one line on what shared budgets do, one on what Google is used for
   (identity only). Then the button.
2. Move out of primary nav (see §E).

**Priority: High** (if kept) / **Moot** (if cut per `APP_STORE.md`)

### B6. Settings — `src/screens/Settings.tsx`

**Purpose:** Income, budgets, categories, data, appearance.

**What is confusing**
- **[Confirmed]** **Appearance (four large theme cards) consumes the entire first viewport.** Income
  and Monthly Budgets — the settings that define the whole app's behaviour — are below the fold,
  beneath a cosmetic feature. This is exactly backwards.
- **[Confirmed]** The **API section is completely unstyled**: a raw browser `<input placeholder="Bearer token">`
  and a default grey `Save token` button 25px tall. It looks like a debug panel that shipped. It also
  offers a bearer token field to a non-technical user with zero explanation of what it is or where to
  get one.
- **[Confirmed]** `Export as CSV`, `Import CSV`, and `Reset This Month's Data` are three adjacent
  pills with identical geometry. The destructive one is distinguished only by red text. **Colour-only
  differentiation on a destructive action.**
- **[Confirmed]** `Save Budgets` sits mid-scroll, is not sticky, and there is no unsaved-changes
  indicator. Edit a budget, scroll to check another, navigate away — changes lost, silently.
- **[Confirmed]** Settings replaces the entire app shell including the tab bar (`App.tsx:30-36`).
  It is a screen pretending to be a modal. Browser/OS back does nothing.
- **[Corrected 2026-07-10]** An earlier draft claimed 11 unlabeled `<input>` elements here. That was a
  false positive in the measuring probe, which checked `aria-label` and text content but never
  `<label for>`. Re-checked properly: every budget input **is** correctly labelled. `category-edit-btn`
  is **24×24px** — that finding stands. **[Confirmed]**
- **[Confirmed]** `Reset This Month's Data` uses a native `confirm()` (`Settings.tsx:234`).

**Recommended changes**
1. Reorder: **Income → Monthly Budgets → Categories → Data → Appearance → Advanced (API)**.
2. Style the API block, or move it behind an "Advanced" disclosure with a one-line explanation and a
   link to the Shortcut setup. **[Assumption]** most users should never see it.
3. Make `Save Budgets` a sticky footer that appears only when the form is dirty, labelled
   `Save changes`. Warn on navigate-away when dirty.
4. Separate destructive actions into their own group with a divider and a heading (`Danger zone`).
   Add an icon + full-width outline treatment, not just red text.
5. Give each budget input a real `<label>`; grow edit buttons to 44px hit areas.
6. Make Settings keep the tab bar, or make it a true sheet with a scrim.

**Priority: Critical** (unsaved changes loss, API block) / **High** (ordering, destructive grouping)

## C. User-Flow Findings

### C1. Add a normal transaction — *the app's core loop*
- **Entry:** Tab bar → Add. (Or Shortcut deep link `?add=true`, `App.tsx:16`.)
- **Current:** Add → tap digits → *(optional category)* → *(optional note)* → Save → lands on Home. **3–5 taps.**
- **Friction:** None significant. This flow is good.
- **Missing feedback:** Save navigates silently. No confirmation the entry was recorded, no undo, no
  indication whether it synced.
- **Recommended:** Same taps, plus a `Saved · Lunch · S$5.80 · Undo` toast on Home. **3–5 taps.**
- **Benefit:** Confidence. Closes the loop the user opened.

### C2. Add a transaction for *yesterday*
- **Entry:** None discoverable from Add Entry.
- **Current:** Add (realise there's no date) → guess → History → scroll → find calendar → tap the day
  → scroll to the "Add missed expense" form → tap Amount → **type on a raw keyboard** → category →
  Save. **8–10 taps + keyboard.**
- **Friction:** The capability is hidden inside a tab named "History". Different input method.
- **Recommended:** Add → tap `Today ▾` → tap yesterday → digits → Save. **5–6 taps.**
- **Benefit:** Removes an entire duplicate form (~200 lines of `History.tsx`) and a whole class of
  "how do I…" confusion.

### C3. Edit a transaction
- **Current:** History → scroll ~1,600px → find entry → tap → inline edit → Save. **4 taps + long scroll.**
- **Friction:** The list, the actual content of "History", is the last thing on the screen.
- **Recommended:** History opens on the list → tap entry → edit → Save. **4 taps, no scroll.**

### C4. Delete a transaction
- **Current:** As C3, then Trash → **Undo toast**. Good.
- **Keep this.** It's the strongest interaction pattern in the codebase.

### C5. Check remaining budget — *the second core loop*
- **Current:** Open app → read Home. **0 taps.**
- **Friction:** The hero shows *spent*, not *remaining*. The remaining figures are in small green text
  on each category row at **4.15:1 contrast**. The most-wanted number is the least prominent.
- **Recommended:** Hero = `S$X left of S$1,200`, with spent as the secondary line. **0 taps, correct answer.**

### C6. Create / change a budget
- **Current:** Home → 34px gear → scroll past four theme cards → tap field → keyboard → … → find
  `Save Budgets` mid-scroll → Save. **6+ taps, high abandonment risk (unsaved changes are lost silently).**
- **Recommended:** Gear (44px) → Income/Budgets are the first thing on screen → edit → sticky
  `Save changes` appears → Save. **4 taps, no data loss.**

### C7. Set up automatic tracking (Apple Pay / DBS Shortcut)
- **Current:** **This flow does not exist in the app.** Setup instructions live only in `README.md`.
  The in-app surface is an unstyled, unexplained `Bearer token` field.
- **Friction:** Total. The app's most distinctive feature is undiscoverable from inside the app.
- **Recommended:** Settings → `Automatic tracking` → a 3-step explainer with the token pre-generated
  and a copy button, plus an `Add to Shortcuts` deep link. **[Assumption]** this is the single highest-leverage
  addition available; it's the feature that differentiates this from every other budget app.

### C8. Handle a failed sync
- **Current:** **No flow.** `EntriesContext.tsx` swallows the error (`catch {}` ×3). The user is never
  told. The entry lives in localStorage and *may* flush later.
- **Recommended:** Persistent, non-blocking status: `2 entries not synced · Retry`. Show it on Home.
- **This is a Critical trust defect, not a polish item.**

### C9. Cancel midway
- **Current:** Add Entry has no cancel — tap another tab and state is discarded with no warning.
  Settings discards edits silently. **[Confirmed]**
- **Recommended:** Add Entry: discarding a partial amount is fine (cheap to redo). Settings: warn.

## E. Navigation Audit

**Current:** `Home · Add · History · Poker · Shared` (bottom tab bar, `TabBar.tsx`)
Settings is a full-screen takeover reached from a 34px gear (`App.tsx:30`).

**Problems**
1. **[Confirmed]** Poker and Shared occupy 40% of primary nav; `docs/APP_STORE.md` cuts both from the
   App Store build. You are shipping nav real estate to features you intend to delete.
2. **[Confirmed]** Settings has no persistent home — it destroys the tab bar and has no OS-back
   integration. It is the *only* route to income, budgets, categories, data export, and the automation
   token, yet it's a 34px icon.
3. **[Confirmed]** "History" is the wrong label for a screen that leads with a data-entry form.
4. **[Confirmed]** `Add` is a tab, not a floating action. It works, but it means the Add screen
   replaces context rather than overlaying it — which is why there's no Cancel.

**Recommended structure**

```
Home  ·  History  ·  [ + ]  ·  Insights  ·  Settings
                      ^ center FAB / prominent tab
```

- **Home** — remaining budget, category rows, sync status.
- **History** — the transaction list first, with search/filter/calendar as controls on it.
- **+ (Add)** — center, visually prominent. Present it as a sheet so Cancel is meaningful.
- **Insights** — the Weekly Spending / Month Review / Day-pattern blocks currently buried in History.
  They deserve their own home and are what make the app feel smart.
- **Settings** — promote out of the 34px gear. It contains everything structural.

**Promote:** Settings (from a 34px icon to a nav item). Insights (from mid-History to its own tab).
Automatic-tracking setup (from `README.md` into the app).
**Demote:** Poker → a Settings entry, or its own build. Shared → a Settings entry until it earns a tab.
**Combine:** "Add missed expense" **into** Add Entry.
**Remove:** the dead `entryToEditId` prop chain (`App.tsx:23`, `:45`).

## H. Recommended Ideal Experience

**1. Brand-new user.** Opens to a single card: *"Track where your S$1,200 goes."* → `Set up my budget`
(pre-filled with sensible defaults; skippable) → lands on Home with an empty state that says
*"Log your first expense"*. First useful result in under 30 seconds, no account required. Offer
automatic tracking only *after* the third manual entry — the user now understands what it automates.

**2. Returning daily user.** Opens to Home. Reads one number: `S$412 left`. Colour and a single line
tell them whether they're on pace. Closes the app. Zero taps.

**3. Quickly recording a purchase.** Shortcut deep link, or tap `+`. Numpad. Two taps of category.
Save. A toast confirms `Saved · Lunch · S$5.80 · Undo`. Four seconds, no keyboard, and they *know* it
saved.

**4. Reviewing monthly spending.** Taps `Insights`. Sees the month's shape — where it went, which days
were heavy, how it compares to last month — in one scroll, with no data-entry forms in the way.

**5. Setting up automatic tracking.** Settings → `Automatic tracking` → a three-step card: what it
does, what it can see, `Add to Shortcuts`. Token generated and copied for them. A live status row
afterwards: `Connected · last synced 2 min ago`.
